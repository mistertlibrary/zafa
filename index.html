MISSION BRIEFING: BEFORE YOU ENTER

You're about to explore five moments in Dominican history that most history textbooks skip or sanitize. This isn't an accident. These events—the arrival of Europeans in 1492, the Trujillo dictatorship, the Parsley Massacre, the Mirabal Sisters' assassination, the 1965 U.S. invasion—are the bones underneath The Brief Wondrous Life of Oscar Wao. You can't understand the novel without understanding this history. You can't understand Oscar, or Beli, or Lola, or the narrator without understanding where they came from.

The Structure:
This is a jigsaw activity. Each of you will become an expert on one historical moment. You'll explore primary sources, read archival materials, and then teach the rest of the class what you learned. Your mission is to make your assigned moment REAL—not just dates and names, but actual human stakes.

The Cosmology:
The novel operates on a system of curses and counterspells. The fukú americanus is the curse, the weight of colonial history that crushes people across generations. The zafa is the counterspell, the resistance, the refusal to let the curse have the last word. As you research, pay attention to both forces. Where do you see the fukú operating? Where do you see people creating zafa?

Content Warning:
This history includes genocide, dictatorship, sexual violence, and imperial occupation. It's brutal because the events were brutal. We're not sanitizing it. If you need to step out, step out. But this is the history that shapes the present. It's not optional.
